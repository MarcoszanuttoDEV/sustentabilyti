Water from the Tietê River in Osasco, Barueri and Parnaíba is considered terrible



Five recycling points and dozens of mobile trash cans installed throughout the city allow residents of Santana de Parnaíba to dispose of rubble and trash properly. This practice contributes to the environment and frees residents from penalties and fines, as the irregular disposal of waste is considered an environmental crime. According to the entity, the water quality of the river was classified as “poor” in 131 km and “terrible” in another 76 km. This data corresponds to 35.9% of the 576 km monitored out of the total 1,100 km, which go from the source in Salesópolis (SP) to Barra Bonita (SP), in the interior of the state.





In the stretch of the cities of Osasco, Barueri and Santana de Parnaíba, the water was considered “terrible”. While in the municipalities of Pirapora do Bom Jesus and Araçariguama, the water was considered “Poor”.
