Sustainability in Environmental Preservation

Environmental
Sustainability

Sustainability is the conscious and responsible use of natural resources, ensuring that the needs of the present are met without compromising future generations. In the face of environmental challenges, such as global warming and the degradation of ecosystems, it is essential to highlight this issue. Preserving the environment must be a priority, as nature plays a fundamental role in maintaining the balance of the planet. Without sustainable measures, negative impacts will continue to grow, affecting biodiversity, the climate and the quality of life of the population. Therefore, promoting sustainability is not just a choice, but an urgent need to ensure a healthier and more balanced future for everyone.

Tamboré Environmental Reserve: Tamboré Environmental Reserve - Santana de Parnaíba


The Tamboré Biological Reserve is an environmental conservation unit located in Santana de Parnaíba, São Paulo, with more than 3.5 million square meters of preserved Atlantic Forest. Established by Municipal Law No. 2,689 on December 22, 2005, its objective is to fully protect the biota and natural attributes, allowing only environmental education and scientific research activities. Considered one of the largest municipal reserves in urban areas in Brazil, it is located in the Alphaville region. Management is the responsibility of the municipal administration, aiming to keep the native fauna and flora protected from human interference and environmental changes.



Negative aspects of sustainability: X

Santana de Parnaíba is one of the cities that suffers most from deforestation in Brazil, mainly due to rapid real estate and population expansion. Urban growth has impacted important natural areas, putting local biodiversity and ecosystems essential for environmental balance at risk. Disorganized occupation can lead to the loss of native vegetation, soil degradation, and compromise of water resources. Therefore, it is essential that there are public policies and initiatives aimed at sustainable development, ensuring that the city's growth occurs in a balanced manner, without harming the environment. In addition, urban expansion increases the improper dumping of waste and garbage, causing the city to have a stretch of the river that runs through it with high levels of pollution, with water levels considered alarming. The implementation of reforestation projects, preservation of green areas, and incentives for sustainable construction can help minimize the impacts of urbanization, ensuring a more sustainable future for the city and its inhabitants.
